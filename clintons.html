The Clintons: Read the Record

Few names draw more heat or more myth from both directions. So the lesson is the discipline: separate what is on the record (votes, laws, verdicts, investigations) from what is asserted (motive, conspiracy, sainthood). Cite the source; grade the claim; skip the legend.

The traps (both directions)

Trap 1 — the conspiracy reflex. "Everyone knows the Clintons did X" is not evidence. Acquitted is acquitted; not charged is not charged. If a claim needs a secret you can't source, it's a story, not a fact.
Trap 2 — the halo. "They were on the right side, so the record doesn't matter" is the same error wearing the other jersey. The crime bill and welfare reform are real votes with real consequences people still debate.
Trap 3 — collapsing two people. Bill and Hillary have distinct records, offices, and votes. "The Clintons" is a convenient blur; the engine grades individuals and specific claims.

The four verdicts (same engine)

TRUE — supported by the record.
FALSE — contradicted by the record.
MISLEADING — real fact, deceptive frame.
INSUFFICIENT — rumor, motive, or forecast.
DECLASSIFIED is openly partisan about its conclusions — and ruthlessly neutral about its method. On a name this loaded, the method is the whole gift: an acquittal and an "extremely careless" are both in the record, and so is a budget surplus. Read all three. Then nobody owns your verdict.
