The Pardon Power: Follow It Yourself

Pardons are one of the few truly unchecked powers in the Constitution — and one of the most public. So the lesson is a method: understand the power, then track any pardon (yes, including January 6) to its primary source and grade it yourself. This page documents the record and hands you the verdict. It is not a hall of shame, and it never names a person to shame them — that's the hit reel this whole site refuses.

What the power is — Article II

The textArt. II, §2: the President "shall have Power to grant Reprieves and Pardons for Offenses against the United States, except in Cases of Impeachment."CONSTITUTION
Federal onlyIt reaches federal offenses. A president cannot pardon a state crime — that's a governor's power, varying by state.SETTLED
Broad & largely unreviewableCourts generally don't second-guess it (Ex parte Garland, 1866). It can even precede a conviction or charge — as in Ford's 1974 pardon of Nixon.CASE LAW

The checklist — track ANY pardon to its source

This is the tool you asked for: a repeatable method, not a verdict handed down. Run it on a pardon from any president, any era.

01Find the grant. Locate the presidential proclamation or the Office of the Pardon Attorney clemency entry. That's the primary record that a pardon happened.
02Find the underlying case. Pull the federal court docket (PACER / CourtListener) for the conviction or charge being pardoned. Conviction records are public.
03State the fact, not the verdict. "X was pardoned on DATE for OFFENSE (cite the docket)" is a checkable fact. "X is guilty/innocent/a hero/a villain" is your judgment — keep it labeled as yours.
04Apply the engine. Grade specific claims about the pardon (was it within the power? was a stated reason accurate?) — TRUE / FALSE / MISLEADING / INSUFFICIENT.

January 6 — same method, no exceptions

Pardons and commutations connected to January 6, 2021 are public record, and the underlying convictions are public court dockets. So you don't need anyone — including this site — to characterize them for you: run the checklist. Find the proclamation; pull the dockets; cite the page. We will not publish a list of names with a "shame" stamp attached, because (a) named ≠ guilty ≠ deserving-of-a-mob (the Epstein lesson), and (b) a curated shame reel is the exact distortion Lesson Mode exists to refuse. The record is open; the conscience is yours.

Controversial pardons are bipartisan — by design of the power

Because the power is nearly unlimited, every era has contested pardons: Ford–Nixon (1974), Carter's draft-evader amnesty (1977), George H.W. Bush's Iran-Contra pardons (1992), Clinton–Marc Rich (2001), and modern grants across administrations. The honest frame isn't "their pardons are corrupt and ours are mercy" — it's "this power has almost no brakes, so watch all of it, and cite the record every time."

TRUE — the proclamation/docket says so.
FALSE — the record contradicts it.
MISLEADING — real grant, deceptive spin.
INSUFFICIENT — motive or judgment, not a fact.
