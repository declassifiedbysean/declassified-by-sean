Project 2025: Read It Yourself

The single most powerful thing about this document is also the most ignored: it is public, and it is long. Roughly 900 pages anyone can download. So the lesson is simple — don’t let either side read it for you. Here’s what it provably is, where to find the primary text, and how to cite the page instead of the meme.

How to read a 900-page blueprint

01Get the actual document. Search the official site (project2025.org) and the publisher (heritage.org) for the Mandate for Leadership PDF. Read the source, not a screenshot of the source.
02Cite the page. A real claim about this document comes with a chapter and page number. “It says X (p. 412)” is checkable. “It secretly wants Y” is a prediction — different thing, lower tier.
03Separate text from forecast. What the document literally proposes is one question (TRUE/FALSE by reading it). What it would cause if enacted is a second question (a prediction — argue it, but label it).
04Check who’s talking. Authors, partner orgs, and politicians have endorsed or distanced themselves at different times. Track the dated, on-the-record statement, not the vibe.

The traps

Summary laundering. Most people “know” this document only through someone else’s 280-character version of it — from both directions. A paraphrase of a paraphrase is not the text. If a claim won’t give you a page number, downgrade it to INSUFFICIENT until you find it.
“It says” vs. “it means.” Quoting a proposal is fact-checkable. Asserting the hidden motive behind it is interpretation. Both are fair to discuss — but only one is a fact, and conflating them is how a real document becomes a fake fight.
Guilt/credit by association, both ways. A coalition of 100+ groups means any individual did not write all 900 pages, and any candidate’s relationship to it is its own dated, checkable question — not an assumption.

The four verdicts (same engine)

TRUE — the text says it (cite the page).
FALSE — the text doesn’t say it.
MISLEADING — real quote, stripped of context.
INSUFFICIENT — a forecast, not a passage.
DECLASSIFIED is openly partisan about its conclusions — and ruthlessly neutral about its method. The strongest argument you can make about any document is one built entirely from its own pages, with the citations attached. Read it yourself. Then you don’t need me, or anyone, to tell you what it says.
