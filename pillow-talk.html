Pillow Talk: Mike Lindell, Graded

The MyPillow founder became one of the loudest voices claiming the 2020 election was hacked. The lesson here isn't mockery — it's a clean case study in sincerity is not evidence. A man can believe something with his whole chest, spend millions proving it, and still be wrong on the record. Graded by the courts and his own contest, with the limits of each marked precisely.

The claims, graded against the record

ClaimVerdictWhy (sourced, with the limits)
"Lindell's 'cyber data' proved the 2020 election was hacked / votes were flipped."FALSEAt his own 2021 "Prove Mike Wrong" challenge, engineer Robert Zeidman showed the files contained no election data at all; an arbitration panel (including Lindell's own arbitrator) ruled unanimously he'd "unequivocally" disproved it. Limit: the $5M award was later vacated by the 8th Circuit (July 2025) on a procedural ground — how the arbitrators read the contest rules, not the data finding — and is now at the Supreme Court on cert.
"A jury found Lindell defamed a Dominion employee."TRUEIn June 2025 a federal jury found Lindell defamed Eric Coomer (calling him a "traitor"), awarding ~$2.3 million. Limit: that's far below the $62.7M sought, and Lindell says he'll appeal.
"MyPillow itself was found liable in that case."FALSEThe jury did not hold MyPillow/FrankSpeech liable for comments others made on his platforms — which is why Lindell framed the verdict as a partial win.
"Lindell has been convicted of a crime over this."FALSEThese are civil defamation findings, not criminal convictions. The distinction matters — don't upgrade a civil verdict into a felony it isn't.

The traps (both directions)

Trap 1 — the upgrade. Treating a civil defamation loss as a criminal conviction. It isn't one. Precision protects your own credibility.
Trap 2 — the vacatur spin. Claiming the 8th Circuit "proved the data was real." It didn't — it vacated a money award on how the arbitrators applied the contest's own rules, not on the merits of whether the files held election data. Read what a ruling actually decided.
Trap 3 — the mockery dead-end. Dunking on "the pillow guy" is easy and useless. The real, transferable lesson is colder: sincerity isn't evidence. Conviction, spending, and a confident demeanor moved millions — and the data still said nothing. That can be anyone's blind spot, on any team.
