Scientology: The Lens, Not the Label

"Done right" means this: separate the documented record (founding, tax status, a court conviction) from the reported-and-disputed (allegations by ex-members and journalists), apply a structural coercion lens you can use on any group — and never confuse a critique of an organization with contempt for the humans inside it. Sourced, careful, and not a clinical verdict.

The lens — use it on everyone, including your own group

The honest tool isn't a label; it's a structural test you run yourself (the full breakdown lives on the sibling site: Lifton's 8 criteria & the BITE model). The three questions that cut through any group, religious or not:

01Can you ask? Are hard questions welcome, or is doubt treated as a defect in you?
02Can you check? Can you read outside sources freely, or is the outside world framed as the enemy?
03Can you leave? Can you walk away with your relationships and resources intact — or does exit cost you everyone and everything?

"Yes" to all three = a healthy community, whatever it believes. "No" = the alarm — and it's the same alarm for a church, a startup, an MLM, or a political movement. Apply it evenly, or you're not using a lens, you're using a grudge.

The traps (both directions)

Trap 1 — the clinical label as a weapon. Slapping "cult" on a named organization as settled fact is a defamation grenade and lazy thinking. Use the structural questions; cite the record (the France conviction is a fact; an internal practice you can't source is an allegation).
Trap 2 — dismissing all critics. A fraud conviction upheld on appeal and a decade of investigative journalism aren't "just bigots." The record is real and citable.
Trap 3 — contempt for members. The people inside are humans, often kind ones, who deserve the door held open if they ever want out — not mockery. Critique the structure; never the soul.
