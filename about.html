About This Project

The Record Is the Arbiter

DECLASSIFIED is a political and legal fact-checking game built on a single operating principle: the evidentiary record is what matters. Not affiliation. Not narrative. Not the emotional temperature of a claim. What does the documented record — statutes, court decisions, official transcripts, verified government data — actually show?

The game was built to answer that question rigorously, interactively, and without flinching. Each claim in the game is assessed using primary sources. Every verdict — TRUE, FALSE, MISLEADING, or INSUFFICIENT INFO — is anchored to something checkable. The annotations tell you exactly what the record shows and why.

INSUFFICIENT INFO is a first-class verdict — not a hedge, not an abstention. Some claims are structured so that they cannot be verified or falsified by any available evidence. Recognizing that accurately is its own form of analytical precision. The game scores it accordingly.

The Game Arc

DECLASSIFIED spans 11+ years of documented claims across five acts plus a supplementary litigation reference game:

Act I: The Outsider (2015–2016) — Campaign emergence. Birtherism, crowd size inflation, personal attacks on judges. Introduction to the Phillips foundational patterns.
Act II: The Transition (2016–2017) — Post-election acceleration. Inauguration crowd size, early executive orders, media delegitimization. Institutional attacks intensify.
Act III: The Reckoning (2021–2024) — January 6 context, false election claims, institutional crisis. Coming Q2 2026.
Act IV: The Return (2024–2026) — Second term claims. Schedule F, tariff economics, federal workforce restructuring. Introduces Benkler Calibration (impact independent of truth).
Act V: The Crisis (2025–2026) — Constitutional and institutional claims. Judicial attacks, executive overreach, civil service dismantling. The most advanced claims with Benkler scoring.
Record Forge: Pro Se Litigation — Supplementary game. Fact-check claims about federal civil rights litigation (McKendry v. PNC Bank). Advanced evidence assessment for legal claims.

Together, the five acts + Record Forge represent 51 documented claims spanning campaign emergence through ongoing second-term governance. Players build analytical skill progressively — from pattern recognition in Act I to institutional impact assessment in Act V.

Analytical Frameworks

The game uses three original analytical frameworks developed specifically for DECLASSIFIED:

The Bogost Citation Scale
Rates evidentiary quality behind a claim: STRONG (primary source), CAREFUL (institutional consensus), WEAK (contested), INSUFFICIENT (no basis). Named to honor game theorist Ian Bogost's work on procedural rhetoric and verification.
The Phillips Pattern Library
A taxonomy of rhetorical mechanisms — Demonstrable Falsehood, Selective Summary, Inflated Metric, Institutional Delegitimization, Personal Attack, and more. Named to honor pattern-recognition work in public communication analysis. Describes mechanism, not intent.
The Benkler Calibration
A difficulty mode weighting claims by historical impact, persistence, institutional power, and structural resistance to debunking. Named for Yochai Benkler's research on information asymmetry and network-level disinformation propagation. Introduced in Act IV.

These frameworks are original game mechanics. They are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by the scholars whose names they reference.

Verification Standard

Every claim assessed in the game is supported by independently verifiable primary sources. Every case citation in the legal content has been verified against primary legal databases. Every statute quoted is verified verbatim against official federal code. AI-assisted research is used as a starting point for human verification — never as the final word.

If you find a factual error in any game content, please contact us. Corrections are taken seriously and made promptly. The integrity of the record is the entire point.
