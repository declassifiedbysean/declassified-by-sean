## The core loop
Friends generate AI art together. Each player names their piece with conviction. The table
receives it. Play by a fixed number of rounds. Best names win; the forgettable middle loses.

## The scoring law — only humans can score
The AI generates; it never judges. Worth is a human act; the machine has no beholder. The
score is reception recorded as fact — Reception instrumented, not refused. Doxology needs a
soul; you cannot teach praise to a rubric.

## The 18+ adult edition — first build, the engine room
Raunchy, blue, grown-up themes — **no porn.** It is the edition that funds the rest, and it
survives the rails (app store, payment processor) *because* nothing explicit is rendered.
Crude is fine; explicit is the loss-condition, not the content.

## Co-op with a traitor — the Battlestar engine
The table cooperates to keep playing, naming, and scoring. A traitor works to **flip the
table.** Crossing the line flips it: everyone loses, the traitor wins on the flip. That
tension is what makes it a game and not a bit.

## The ToS is the rulebook — radical legibility
Rules, content floor, and consent live in one instrument. Agreeing to play *is* consenting
to the constitution. The ToS does legal work and game-design work in the same breath — and
it is how an 18+ game documents the floor and the consent.

## THE DESIGN LAW (load-bearing) — the violation is the sanction, not the pixel
The traitor must **not** win by making explicit content get produced — that re-arms every
landmine the no-porn rule defuses (store, processor, engine guardrail) and incentivizes
generating the thing that de-platforms you. So: the engine refuses to render porn, by
policy. The crossable line is a *declared ToS boundary*; crossing it is a **social act** —
the table *allowing* or *scoring* something over the floor. The traitor baits; the crew
holds the line and spots the baiter; the flip triggers on **human judgment.** No explicit
content ever needs to exist. The rails hold and the scoring law holds.

## The funding stack — museum and gift shop
The adult edition pays for the rubrics and the lessons. Loud room funds the quiet room; same
building, separate doors. And it is not only a funder — a game about *holding a line under
social pressure and reading intent* teaches the exact skill the curriculum teaches. The gift
shop sells the museum's wisdom, blue.

## The honest tech reality
- The engine is **image generation** — a separate vendor, priced **per image**, the biggest
  line on the cost sheet. Not a free text-token rounding error.
- SFW edition and the no-porn adult edition both run on **compliant image APIs and normal
  processors** — precisely because nothing explicit is rendered.
- An explicit-image version would be the off-rails version: self-hosted GPUs, no store, no
  PayPal. The design specifically avoids it. That is a feature.
- **Image generation is not buildable inside a Claude artifact** (text only). The buildable
  prototype in-artifact is the text-naming core; the image game is the APK lane.
