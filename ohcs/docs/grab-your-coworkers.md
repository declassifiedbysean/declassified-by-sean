# "Grab your coworkers" — the application line

*Professional / portfolio register. The public `how-to.html` keeps the joke universal — one knowing wink, no hiring text — so the site stays a game, not a job application. The fully-aimed version lives here, for the cover letter, where it's appropriate. Same joke, two homes, on purpose.*

**The move:** the portfolio centerpiece is a game that cannot be played by one person. To a hiring reviewer that reads, at first, like a problem — you can't sit at your desk and try it. The line below turns that into the argument. A product premised on human judgment refuses to let a single person decide its worth; evaluating it honestly requires the exact thing it's about. The constraint *is* the demonstration.

---

## Option A — the dry version (recommended)

> The centerpiece of my portfolio, *Only Humans Can Score*, is a same-room game built on a single rule: a machine can paint, guess, and bluff, but only humans confer worth — and never just one human. The smallest table is three. Which means there's no way to evaluate it from one chair. If you want to see whether I can build a product that reasons about human judgment, you'll have to exercise some of your own, together, with at least two colleagues. The game doesn't make exceptions for hiring committees. I'd consider that a fair test of both of us.

## Option B — the bolder version

> I could tell you I build humane products that take human judgment seriously. Instead I built one that proves it by refusing to work without you. *Only Humans Can Score* needs three humans at the table; there is no single-player mode and there never will be, because the whole thesis is that worth is conferred by a room, not a person and not a machine. So here's the unusual ask in a cover letter: don't evaluate the demo alone. Grab two coworkers, take ten minutes, and let the thing argue for itself the only way it's allowed to.

## One-liner — drop anywhere (résumé footer, portfolio header, email)

> Portfolio note: the game can't be played solo — grab two coworkers. That's not a limitation, it's the thesis.

---

## Why it's safe in this register

- **Not political.** It runs entirely on the product and the role. The civic platform stays out of the room, per the lane separation.
- **Competence, not crusade.** It demonstrates the judgment (designing around human-only scoring, building for a table) rather than asserting it.
- **Honest.** The three-human floor is real and enforced in the code; the line doesn't oversell. If anything it under-claims and lets the artifact carry it.

**Calibration:** A reads as confident and self-aware; B reads as memorable but spends more nerve and risks "try-hard" with a stiff committee. GovAI skews thoughtful, so A is the safer premium. Hold B for a warmer, smaller shop where personality is the differentiator.
