Pricing

Play is free and anonymous, for as long as the lights stay on.* The paid Pass is the one door you choose to walk through — the prize economy, the Fuel detection duel, and a place on the wall. Buying a Pass never de-anonymizes free play. That's the keystone term.

* Not “forever” — we don’t overclaim. Earth’s biosphere has roughly 1 billion years before the brightening Sun ends it; the last stars burn out near 100 trillion years from now. The record outlasts the planet, but not the lights.

Not yet on sale. The paid tier opens after the free game launches and the wall is built (counsel, processor, moderation). These are the planned terms — read them, hold us to them.

A Pass also opens the rated ladder — a persistent Elo across games against other Pass holders. Free play stays unrated and anonymous by design: a rating needs an identity to attach to, and free play keeps none.

A 32-seat, single-elimination duel for Pass holders, seeded by your ladder Elo. It runs across the working week — a round a day, Monday to the Friday final — so there's a match to come back for each day.

On select, pre-announced weekends: the marquee event, its own self-contained bracket, with a real trophy for the winner. The big one.

Winners are decided by human votes, counted plays, and head-to-head skill. The machine never judges — in the duel it only measures. Prizes are a skill-based reward program, finalized with counsel before any open. Full terms & prize rules →
