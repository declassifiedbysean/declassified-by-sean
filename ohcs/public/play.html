A Captain feeds the machine a secret. The machine paints it. Everyone names what they see — even the Captain, who's bluffing. And the machine drops its own guess into the lineup, in disguise. You crown the best, the worst, and whoever caught the machine. It can paint. It can guess. It can never score. A machine always plays — even at the smallest table. This is always AI versus human.

The Constitution  —  agreeing to play is agreeing to it
I.The machine always plays. It paints and guesses. Only humans can score.
II.The naming is the value. Name with conviction, or be forgotten.
III.Best and worst both ascend. The mediocre middle is the only loss.
IV.Eighteen and over. This is an adult table.
V.No porn. Cross the line and the table flips — everyone loses.
VI.No faces, no famous. The machine paints things, not people — no real faces, no celebrities, no public figures. A likeness is a right that isn't yours to spend.
VII.The record is kept, depersonalized. What was made and judged is kept anonymously and may be studied; who you are is never kept. Don't type personal details into a name.
VIII.⋯Unratified. The curator's résumé is available upon request. Opening this counted as the request.
