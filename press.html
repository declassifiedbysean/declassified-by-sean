What Is DECLASSIFIED?

DECLASSIFIED is a browser-based educational game in which players evaluate political claims using actual primary sources — congressional records, court decisions, verified government data, official transcripts. Each claim yields a verdict (TRUE, FALSE, MISLEADING, or INSUFFICIENT INFO), a citation tier, and an identified rhetorical pattern.

The game spans 11 years of documented public claims across five acts, from the 2015 campaign through the current second term, plus a supplementary litigation module (Record Forge) applying the same methodology to federal civil rights case documents.

Built solo by Sean McKendry, filed under his real name, playable immediately with no account required.

Press FAQ

Is DECLASSIFIED partisan?
No. The platform evaluates claims against the documented evidentiary record — statutes, court decisions, official transcripts, verified government data. The record is the arbiter, not political affiliation. The current subject matter reflects the documented public record of a specific public figure over a specific documented period.
Who built this?
Sean McKendry, solo. No team, no funding, no institutional backing. Built under his real name as a matter of deliberate accountability.
What is the Benkler Calibration?
A difficulty mode in Acts IV and V that weights claims by historical impact, institutional persistence, and structural resistance to debunking — independent of whether the claim is true or false. Named to honor Yochai Benkler's research on information asymmetry. The harder mode in DECLASSIFIED reflects real-world conditions where false claims with high institutional impact are more consequential than false claims with low impact.
What is Record Forge?
A supplementary game module applying DECLASSIFIED's fact-checking methodology to claims about federal civil rights litigation — specifically McKendry v. PNC Bank, N.A. Players evaluate claims about the case using actual court documents, filings, and statutory text. Advanced evidence assessment for legal claims.
Is there an Act III?
Act III (2021–2024), covering the January 6 context and post-2020 election claims, is in development. Projected release Q2 2026.
Is the site ad-supported?
The site displays contextual advertising through Google AdSense. Advertisers have no influence over game content, claim selection, verdict assignment, or annotation text. Editorial independence is absolute.
