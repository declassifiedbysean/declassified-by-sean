Trump: the Record, Graded (not the insult)

DECLASSIFIED has its opinions — loudly. But "he's a dick, full stop" is a vibe, and a vibe loses arguments and invites lawsuits. So here's the upgrade: the documented record, graded claim-by-claim against current (2026) sources, with the smears in both directions flagged. Convicted is not the same as charged; liable is not the same as guilty; voided-on-appeal is not the same as never-happened. Precision is the weapon. The receipts beat the epithet.

How to actually win this argument

01Name the adjudicated thing. "Convicted felon (34 counts)" and "civilly liable for sexual abuse" are true and citable. "Dick" / "criminal" as loose epithets are not — and the loose ones are where defamation and eye-rolls live.
02Get the legal words right. Convicted ≠ charged ≠ liable ≠ "guilty of everything." The person who says "rapist" flatly loses to the person who says "a civil jury found him liable for sexual abuse" — because the second one can't be knocked down.
03Update for 2026. The fraud penalty was voided; the federal cases were dismissed. Citing them as current is the unforced error that lets the other side dismiss everything you said.

The traps (both directions)

Trap 1 — the epithet as evidence. "He's a dick" is a feeling, not a finding. It persuades no one who isn't already nodding, and slapping an uncharged crime on a named person is a defamation grenade. Cite the conviction; skip the slur.
Trap 2 — charged = guilty. The dismissed federal cases were never tried. Treating an indictment as a verdict is the exact error the other side makes about their enemies. Don't borrow it.
Trap 3 — "witch hunt erases it all." Appeals and dismissals are real and you must report them — but they don't vaporize a jury conviction and two civil findings that currently stand. Sand off nothing in either direction.
