The Record — Lesson Mode

Lesson Mode: How the Record Works

The whole engine, shown in the open. This is how every act grades a claim — and how you can grade one yourself, from any public figure, without taking anyone’s word for it. The method is the lesson; no claim is asserted here that isn’t sourced.

The engine, in four steps

01Isolate a factual claim. Not an opinion, not a vibe — a statement that resolves to true or false. “This is bad” is a belief (and a belief, honestly declared, is not a lie — Article IX). “The number is X / the law says Y / this happened” is checkable.
02Pin it to a primary source. A dated quote, video, transcript, filing, or official record — not a paraphrase of a paraphrase. If you can’t link it, you can’t grade it.
03Choose the evidence tier. How strong is the support — primary record, corroborated reporting, single source, or contested?
04Return a verdict against the evidence, and name the rhetorical pattern if there is one.

The four verdicts

TRUE — supported by the record.
FALSE — contradicted by the record.
MISLEADING — technically defensible, built to deceive.
INSUFFICIENT — the evidence doesn’t resolve it (yet).

The fairness rule

Pick claims by topic, not by “find the worst thing someone ever said.” Cherry-picking is its own distortion — the engine is for assessing the record, not assembling a hit reel. Rhetoric, predictions, and values stay out; they aren’t false, they’re just not the job.
