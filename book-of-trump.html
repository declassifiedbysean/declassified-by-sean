The Book of Trump

This is satire. A parody of rhetorical style and a gallery of opinion — not a record of events. Nothing on this page is asserted as a fact about any real person. The actual claims, the checkable ones, are graded with primary sources in the Acts. Here we just do the voice.

⚠️ LABELED SATIRE. Protected parody and commentary on a public figure. The “verses” below imitate a manner of speaking; they describe no real events and prove no claims. For the sourced record — TRUE / FALSE / MISLEADING / INSUFFICIENT, with citations — see the Acts. (Art. IX: no claim on assertion alone — which is exactly why the facts aren’t here.)

Verses (in the style of)

Superlatives 1:1“And it was the best speech. Many people are saying — the best. Some say the greatest ever given, but I would never say that. I’m very modest. The most modest, believe me.”
Superlatives 3:14“Nobody knew. Nobody knew it could be so complicated. They said it couldn’t be done. I said it could. We’ll see what happens. We’ll see.”
Weave 7:7“So I’m talking about the boats, the sharks, the batteries — tremendous batteries — and I say, sir, would you rather, and they go, sir, sir. Strong men. Tears in their eyes.”

That’s the bit: the cadence is the joke. It’s instantly recognizable precisely because it’s a style, not a fact — which is what makes it parody and not a claim.

The Hall (of opinion)

🏅 Inducted: Achievement in the Superlative

An opinion gallery for rhetorical feats — the “bigly,” the “many people are saying,” the adjective doing the work a fact should. Commentary, not accusation.

🏅 Inducted: The Unfalsifiable “We’ll See”

A masterclass in saying a sentence that can never be graded — which is why this site exists. The ungradeable is the opposite of the record.

Why it’s built this way: satire and the fact-check engine do different jobs. Satire mocks the voice and is honest about being opinion; the Acts grade the claims and are honest about needing sources. Keeping them in separate rooms is how the fact-check keeps its credibility — you can’t launder an opinion into a verdict. Be cool, be nice, be sovereign, be a neighbor (Art. XIII). The record is downstairs.
